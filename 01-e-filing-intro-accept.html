Federal Court of Appeal Electronic Filing System

By checking each box, you confirm that you have read, understood and agree to the linked document.

Welcome screen agreement
    • Key points in the Consolidated Practice Direction
    • As of June 1, 2023, electronic documents filed using this service are official Court records, equal to paper documents.
    • Documents filed online are still subject to Tariff A fees, which you must pay after you receive an e-mail prompt for payment (if applicable). If you pay for the document within three (3) days of submission, the document is considered to have been accepted for filing at the time that you submitted it.
    • If the E-Filing system is down, you can use a back-up e-mail address to submit your document(s) to the Registry.
    • To be accepted for filing, your document(s) must:
      • be in Portable Document Format (*.pdf)
      • be page numbered, labelled and arranged in a way that is logical and accurately describes the contents
      • not be password or security-protected
      • not contain malware, viruses, or any malicious content
      • [not?] have any commenting functionality enabled
    • We also recommend that your document(s):
      • have Optical Character Recognition (OCR) applied
      • use electronic bookmarks for each entry, exhibit, and transcript
      • use hyperlinks to link to relevant acts, regulations, and case law
      • are 100MB in size or less. If you need to send larger document(s), you should break them up or contact the Registry to submit in a different way.
    • You must keep the original paper copies of any documents that you have submitted online until 30 days after all appeal periods have expired.
    • Do not use E-Filing to submit documents subject to a confidentiality order. File paper copies or contact the Registry for assistance.
    • Key points in the Privacy Statement

    By using the E-Filing system, you consent to your information being collected and used in accordance with the Privacy Statement. This includes:

    • Contact information of the person submitting documents: This includes the name, phone number, and email address of the contact who is submitting documents using the E-Filing system. The Courts Administration Service (CAS) does not save information from cancelled or incomplete submissions, and does not disclose or use any of the contact information (except by consent of the individual or if required by law).
    • Information in the documents being submitted: Documents submitted through E-Filing usually become available to the public as online court records. You should take steps to minimize the amount of sensitive and personal information in your documents. The Court and CAS cannot keep third parties from accessing the public court record and misusing the information.
      If a document is subject to a confidentiality order, do not use E-Filing to submit it.
    • Information about the use of the E-Filing system: CAS automatically collects technical information about your use of E-Filing (e.g. time and device used to access the system). This information is not used by CAS to identify you.
    • Processing and storage of information: All information that CAS collects from E-Filing is processed and stored in Canada.
      Although security measures are in place, the Court and CAS cannot guarantee that information collected through E-Filing will never be accessed or misused by an unauthorized third party. If this happens, CAS will notify you.
    • Key points about document preservation
    • You must keep the original paper copies of any signed documents and affidavits that you have submitted online for the longer of:
      • 30 days after all appeal periods have expired
      • 30 days after the Supreme Court has issued any decision in the matter
    • If the Court requests an original document or affidavit, you must be able to provide it. If not, it may be removed from the official Court record.
    • Key points in the Terms of Use

    By using the E-Filing system, you agree to be bound by the E-Filing Terms of Use and all applicable Court laws and policies. If you are using E-Filing on behalf of another person and/or an organization, you are agreeing to the Terms of Use on their behalf. This includes:

    • Documents that you submit using E-Filing are not considered filed until the Registry confirms that they have been accepted (usually by email).
    • The E-Filing system and instructions are offered "as-is." You are responsible for meeting any document filing deadlines, regardless of any system outages or interruptions. The Government of Canada (including CAS), the Court, and their Representatives are not responsible for any damages that you may experience from using the E-Filing system.
    • E-Filing does not provide for service of documents to other parties.
    • If the document(s) you submit require a fee under Tariff A (Federal Courts Rules), they will only be filed after your payment is received. You can pay online by credit card using a secure payment link that the Registry will provide, or other methods of payment.
      • By using the E-Filing system, you represent and confirm that you:
      • are a party, intervenor, or representative of a party/intervenor to the proceeding that you are filing documents for
      • are legally authorized to file documents with the Court, and will follow all applicable rules, policies, orders, directions, etc.
      • are making a choice to use the E-Filing system
      • understand that you should submit document(s) in Portable Document Format (*.pdf)
      • are solely responsible for filing your document(s), meeting deadlines, and getting the authorizations you need to submit documents on behalf of others
      • are solely responsible for having the working devices and internet connection needed to use the E-Filing system
    • You will not use (or allow anyone else to use) the E-Filing system to:
      • violate, or encourage anyone to violate, these Terms of Use or any laws or rules
      • prevent anyone else from using E-Filing
      • copy, distribute, or mimic the E-Filing system and/or instructions
      • spread a virus, worm, malicious code or anything else that might damage or disrupt the E-filing system or hardware
      • scrape data, documents, or information
      • misrepresent your identity or representation of another person
      • violate the rights of another person
      • do things that are hateful, harassing, discriminatory
      • otherwise interfere with, disrupt, or limit the operation or functionality of the E-Filing system.
    • By providing your email address to the E-Filing System, you allow CAS to contact you by email in connection with the E-Filing System and the Court process.
    • CAS may update these Terms of Use at its sole discretion at any time, without notifying you. You are responsible for reviewing these Terms of Use each time before you use the E-Filing System.
    • By using the E-Filing system, you agree to be bound by the applicable laws of Ontario and Canada in your use of the system.
    • CAS may end any feature of the E-Filing system without notifying you.
