Submit documents to the Federal Court online

Overview

A party, or a party's legal representative, can securely submit documents to the Federal Court online using this application. This is also known as Electronic filing, or E-Filing. You can submit documents for new or existing proceedings in all areas of the Federal Court's jurisdiction.

Documents for the Federal Court of Appeal will not be accepted for filing through this E-Filing Portal. Please submit at the Federal Court of Appeal.

The Federal Court recommends that you review the E-filing Guide and resources before you begin your submission.

Start submission

Before you submit

To start your document submission, have the following available:

  • the formatted document(s) that you wish to submit and proof that they have been served (if applicable)
    •   Document formatting guidelines

    • All documents must be in Portable Document format (PDF files)
    • Documents cannot be password-protected
    • Documents must be formatted (fonts, page numbers, etc.) according to the format for printed documents in the Federal Court Rules
    • Any typed text in documents must be searchable
    • Documents cannot be longer than 500 pages
    • You should insert bookmarks in the file anywhere that you would include a physical tab in a paper filing
  • an e-mail address and phone number that the Registry can use to contact you or your contact person
  • a stable internet connection (the application cannot save partial submissions)
  • the application open in only one (1) browser tab at a time
  • the Court File Number / Court Number, if you are planning to submit documents on an existing proceeding

You have the ability to identify that a filing is urgent, to include special handling instructions for a document, and to choose your Registry office for filing.

Do not submit documents subject to a confidentiality order or direction online. Follow the steps for submitting confidential documents.

If you are filing document(s) less than 5 days before your scheduled hearing, do not submit online. You will need to provide paper copies to the Court.

Serving documents and proof of service

Submitting documents online does not serve documents to other parties. For most documents under the Federal Court Rules, you must provide copies of the document(s) to the responding party/parties before filing with the Court and provide proof of service. Consult the Federal Court Rules for information on how to serve documents, proof of service, and deadlines.

Serving documents electronically (e-service)

You may serve documents electronically to the responding party(ies) if they are not required to be served personally under the Federal Court Rules.

Before serving your document(s) electronically, confirm that the responding parties have provided their consent to electronic service for the proceeding through the Notice of Consent to Electronic Service (Form 141A). You will need to provide a copy of their signed Form 141A with your document submission.

Note that if you serve a document (other than an originating document or a warrant) on a Saturday, Sunday, holiday, or after 5:00 p.m. at the recipient's local time; it is deemed to be served on the following business day. Holidays are defined in section 35(1) of the Interpretation Act.

Consult the deadline guidelines for information on service deadlines.

Proof of service

If you are submitting a document that is not an originating document through E-Filing, you must also submit a document containing proof that it has been served within the appropriate timeline.

If you served paper documents

If you are submitting a document that is not an originating document through E-Filing, your submission must include proof that the document has been served within the appropriate timeline.

If you served documents electronically

To prove that you have served document(s) electronically, your submission must include an Affidavit of Service (Form 146A) or a Solicitor’s Certificate of Service (Form 146B), with:

  • confirmation that the document(s) were served electronically
  • e-mail or secure link ("electronic address") to which you served the document(s)
  • the manner in which you validated that the document was served, with proof attached. Refer to 4.4.3 of the E-Filing Guide for acceptable proof
  • a copy of a signed Form 141A.

Online access to Court records

Documents available online

As of September 12, 2022, all non-confidential documents of certain types or practice areas that are submitted and filed online will be made available for online access:

  • Aboriginal Law, Maritime and Admiralty, and Intellectual Property practice areas
  • class actions
  • pleadings (e.g., statements of claim and defence, notices of application, notices of motion, notices of appeal)
  • written arguments (written representations, memoranda of fact and law)
  • Court-generated documents (e.g. orders, directions, reasons, judgments) related to the above matters.

These documents will be available for online access three (3) business days after they are filed.

Documents subject to a confidentiality order or exemption from online access are the only exceptions.

Request exemption

You can request that all, or part of, certain documents be exempt from online access. To do so:

  1. Submit the document(s) online through E-Filing, and indicate that you are seeking an online exemption.
  2. In your submission, include a letter with an informal request for exemption as one of your documents.
  3. A member of the Court will review the request, and determine whether the document(s) are made available online. If request for exemption is granted, the Court may require you to file an alternative version of the document that can be posted online.

Confidential documents

Do not use E-Filing to submit documents that are subject to a confidentiality order or direction. Submit confidential documents to the Court by e-mail or paper.

You can still submit the non-confidential documents related to the proceeding using the application.

Submit by e-mail

  1. Clearly identify your document(s) as 'Confidential'.
  2. Save your document(s) as a password-protected PDF file, or upload to a secure file transfer service.
  3. E-mail the PDF or document link to your local Registry office.
    • PDF attachments sent by e-mail must be smaller than 18 MB, so you may have to split large documents into multiple parts before sending. You may consult our advice for limiting file size (p. 14).
  4. Provide the password or instructions to access the document(s) to your local Registry office in a separate e-mail or by phone.

Submit paper copies

  1. Find your local Registry Office location.
  2. Bring your document(s) in person during their office hours, or send by mail.

Urgent submissions

You can use the e-filing portal to submit and flag urgent document(s), but you are strongly advised to contact the Registry before e-filing document(s) on urgent matters.

If you are filing document(s) for a hearing scheduled in less than five (5) days, you must file by paper with at your local Registry office.

Submitting document(s) through e-filing does not guarantee that it will be processed immediately. If you submit your urgent document(s) outside of regular business hours, contact the Registry office where you submitted them to ensure timely processing.

How to use E-Filing

To submit your document(s) for filing, you must complete all steps in a single session. It is not possible to save an incomplete session. The session will automatically time out after 60 minutes of inactivity, without warning.

  1. Ensure that you have served the document(s) to the other party or parties, if applicable. For information, review: Serving document(s)
  2. Submit your formatted PDF document(s), using the step-by-step instructions in the application. For confidential documents, follow the steps for submitting confidential documents.
  3. Immediately after you submit, you will receive a unique confirmation number on the website and by e-mail. Keep this number in case you need to follow up on your document status.
  4. The Registry will review your document:
    • If it is accepted for filing by the Registry, you will receive an e-mail verifying acceptance and containing a link to pay your filing fee (if the fee applies).
    • If it is not accepted for filing by the Registry, you will receive an e-mail with further instructions.
    • If you do not receive a response from the Registry after you submit, contact your local Registry office with your submission confirmation number.

If you encounter a problem with the Electronic Filing System, you should contact the Registry at 1-800-663-2096 between 8:30 a.m. to 4:30 p.m., Monday to Friday except on holidays.

You should keep a copy of all documents sent electronically for 30 days after the expiry of all appeal periods. Also keep the original paper version of any document(s) that you have scanned.

Fees and payment

There are no transaction fees to use the E-Filing Portal. However, fees may apply to file certain document types. Filing fees are available in IMM Rule 23 (for immigration appeals) and Tariff A (for all other documents).

If a filing fee is required, the Registry will send an e-mail requesting online payment to the e-mail address provided in your submission with a link and the amount to be paid.

Online payment can only be made by credit card. If you need to pay with a different method, please call the Registry.

How to pay your fees online

  1. Use the payment link provided in the e-mail from the Registry to access the Pay Now website, and enter the required information for your submission:
    • the reference number
    • the e-mail address on file for the submission
  2. Review the pre-populated screen information in the Confirm Payment Details screen. This information cannot be edited, so please contact the Registry if it is incorrect.
  3. Provide your credit card information and submit. The cardholder does not have to be the submission contact.
  4. If payment is successful, you will get a confirmation screen and e-mail. If the payment is declined or cancelled, you will need to call the Registry to request a new payment link e-mail or to use another method.

Filing deadlines

Documents that you submit for filing through the E-Filing Portal are not considered successfully filed by the Registry until the document(s) have been reviewed and payment of document fee(s) are received (if applicable). When a document is submitted and is accepted by a Registry Officer, the document is deemed filed at the date/time it was submitted (EST) unless submitted on a holiday.

If you submit your document(s) on a holiday, it will be deemed filed the next business day. For the purposes of the Rules, “holiday” means a Saturday, Sunday or any other day defined as a holiday in subsection 35(1) 35(1) of the Interpretation Act.

All filing deadlines apply to document(s) filed through the E-Filing portal. If you miss a filing deadline due to technical problems, you need to request an extension of time by contacting the Registry. There is no guarantee that an extension will be granted.
