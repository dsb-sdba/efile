Federal Court of Appeal Electronic Filing System

Step 1: Court File

Please select the Language, Type, Subject and Nature applicable to this New Proceeding. You can also enter your internal office file number if you wish to do so for your own file management purposes.




  1. Appeal (s. 27 of the Federal Courts Act or statutory appeal set out in any other act): a statutory remedy through which a party may challenge a lower court decision or a tribunal’s decision before the Federal Court of Appeal. A party who initiates an appeal is named "appellant"; the one against which the appeal is formed is named "respondent" (rule 338).
  2. Application for judicial review (s. 28 of the Federal Courts Act): a mechanism that allows a party to ask the Federal Court of Appeal to review administrative decisions to ensure that the government and its agencies acted reasonably, fairly and within the power granted to them. A party who initiates an application for judicial review is named "applicant" ; the one against which it is formed is named "respondent" (rule 303).
  3. Reference: is a procedure by which the Attorney General of Canada or an administrative tribunal asks the Federal Court of Appeal for its opinion on an issue that it considers important (s. 28(2) and s. 18.3).
  4. Preliminary: is a procedure that allows a party to ask the Federal Court of Appeal to decide on any preliminary matter that must be resolved before a file can be opened (ie. motion for leave to appeal, request for extension of time, etc.).

Please click the "Add Party" button above to enter the names of the parties involved in this proceeding.
For most proceedings, you must enter the names of a minimum of two opposing parties.
For example:
In an application for judicial review, enter the names of the Applicant(s) and the Respondent(s).
In an appeal, enter the names of the Appellant(s) and the Respondent(s).
In a motion, enter the names of the Moving Party or Parties and the Respondent(s).
In a reference, there may only be one applicant. Please enter the name of this party.

Step 3: Add Document

Please add information about your document(s) to be submitted. Use the Add another document button to add any additional document(s).

Attach the file that you would like to upload and complete the details for each document.

Documents must:

  • be in PDF (Portable Document Format, .pdf) format
  • have any security settings deactivated
  • not contain malware, viruses, or other malicious content
  • have any “commenting” functionality enabled
  • be 100MB in size or smaller
    • If your document is over 100MB, we recommend using software to reduce the size or contacting the Registry to make alternate arrangements.

Include any special instructions for using and storing the document under Document Handling Instructions.

DO NOT UPLOAD CONFIDENTIAL DOCUMENTS TO THE E-FILING SYSTEM.

Submitting Confidential Electronic Documents

Do not use the E-filing system to submit confidential electronic documents. Instead, contact the Registry to make filing arrangements.

You might also want to consult Rules 151-152 of the Federal Courts Rules and the Court's consolidated practice direction to learn more about confidential documents and the Court's practice around them.
